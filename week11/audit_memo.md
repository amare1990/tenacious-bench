# Audit Memo — Week 10 Evidence Gap Analysis

Week 10 evidence shows Tenacious needs a domain-specific critic, not a generic retail-agent benchmark. τ²-Bench retail primarily measures whether an agent can follow a tool-mediated customer-service trajectory: look up orders, apply policy, update state, and finish without invalid tool calls. Tenacious-specific behavior requires something different: evidence-grounded outbound judgment under uncertainty, including signal confidence, capacity limits, pricing-policy boundaries, tone, and single-ask outreach discipline.

The probe library exposed dimension-level failures across at least eight probes: `P-001_missing_signal_reference`, `P-002_weak_signal_overassertion`, `P-003_pricing_violation`, `P-004_bench_overcommitment`, `P-005_banned_phrase`, `P-006_generic_template`, `P-007_condescending_gap`, `P-008_fabricated_signal`, `P-009_multi_ask`, and `P-010_ai_maturity_misroute`. These are not generic “bad email” issues; they map to specific Tenacious risk dimensions: evidence grounding, epistemic restraint, commercial-policy compliance, capacity realism, brand voice, personalization, respectful framing, factuality, CTA control, and routing.

Trace evidence shows the same pattern in operational runs. `trace-001` and `trace-002` contain usable lead context but weak signal selection. `trace-003` and `trace-004` show candidate messages that overstate hiring pressure from thin evidence. `trace-005` surfaces internal bench language leaking into prospect-facing copy. `trace-006` shows unsupported capacity confidence. `trace-007` exposes generic-template drift despite available company context. `trace-008` demonstrates multi-ask outreach that dilutes the next step. `trace-009` shows pricing/commitment language beyond approved policy. `trace-010` shows AI-maturity framing that risks sounding condescending.

The gap is therefore not benchmark coverage in the abstract. It is a judge calibration gap: the system can produce fluent copy, but it lacks a reliable critic for Tenacious-specific acceptability. Path B addresses that gap by training a preference critic to reject outputs that are syntactically plausible but fail on one of the Tenacious dimensions above. The benchmark must consequently include trace-derived failures, parameterized counterfactuals, synthesis-generated edge cases, and hand-authored adversarial cases.
